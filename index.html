Interests

My research focuses on realizing autonomy and functionality in soft physical machines by leveraging mechanical and fluidic nonlinearities as well as material properties. I believe that autonomous machines exploiting emergent phenomena arising from physical interactions within their structures and with the surrounding environment can open new possibilities beyond what conventional robots—based on centralized control—can achieve. I am also interested in implementing the remarkable functions and capabilities observed in living organisms by exploring the underlying structural principles that govern biological systems.

Research highlights

Harnessing Self-Oscillating Fluid-Structure Interactions to Design Emergent Autonomy in Physical Machines

Under review

By harnessing the fluid-structure interaction phenomena of soft fluidic self-oscillating actuators, autonomous behaviors can emerge in electronics-free robots—and their swarms—without relying on electronic hardware or software-based control.

Under Review

A Canine Musculoskeletal Robot for Investigating Biomechanical Functions During Locomotion

Advanced Robotics Research, 2025

Using thin McKibben artificial muscles that are lightweight, highly compliant, and capable of contracting even when bent, we built a musculoskeletal canine-limb robot and showed that a hammock-like connection structure between the forelimbs and the trunk contributes to improved walking efficiency.

Read on WILEY Youtube

McKibben Artificial Muscle Embedded with Stretchable Textile Sensor

Advanced Intelligent Systems, 2025

By embedding a resistive stretchable textile sensor inside a McKibben artificial muscle, we can measure displacement without adding any external sensing components to the actuator.

Read on WILEY

McKibben Muscle With Elastic Thread Embedded in Parallel Extending Range of Motion of Muscle-Driven Robots

IEEE Robotics and Automation Letters, 2025

By integrating, in parallel with a McKibben artificial muscle, an add-on component that enables muscle-like passive elongation under external loads when inactive (back-stretching), we can increase the antagonistic actuation range. This is the new method proposed in our 2023 paper.

Read on IEEE Youtube

Serially Coupled Self-Excited Pneumatic Actuator for Environment-Adaptive Steering Robot

IEEE Robotics and Automation Letters, 2024

By fluidically coupling two self-oscillating actuators, each actuator can modulate its oscillation characteristics in response to physical interactions with the surroundings. By embedding this nontrivial physical phenomenon into soft physical machines, they can autonomously adapt to environmental changes and achieve self-steering behavior.

Read on IEEE Youtube

Back-Stretchable McKibben Muscles: Expanding the Range of Antagonistic Muscle Driven Joints

IEEE Robotics and Automation Letters, 2023

By implementing muscle-like passive elongation under external loads—back-stretching, where the actuator is naturally stretched beyond its rest length when inactive—into a McKibben artificial muscle, we can increase the antagonistic actuation range.
